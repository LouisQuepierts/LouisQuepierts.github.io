Game Design & Development

About Me

A game developer in formation, currently studying Software Engineering at Guangdong University of Foreign Studies (expected 2026). Early experimentation with Minecraft mods and the recreation of mechanics from other games sparked an enduring curiosity about the relationship between code, design, and human experience.

Game creation is approached not merely as a technical or visual endeavor, but as a medium for exploring aesthetics, interaction, and the essence of human connection. Projects such as ThatSkyInteractions Mod (TeaCon award-winning), Phos, and other prototypes serve as platforms to investigate how subtle mechanics, environmental design, and player interaction shape emotional resonance.

Beyond development, engagement with music production (FL Studio), 3D design (Blender/BlockBench), and linguistic exploration (Japanese) reflects a belief that diverse creative practices enrich the potential of interactive media. The long-term vision centers on establishing a studio where technology, artistry, and philosophy converge, while continuing to experiment, collaborate, and expand the expressive boundaries of games.

Journey

Spark

First Inspirations

Early experimentation with Minecraft mods sparked curiosity about the interplay of mechanics, creativity, and player interaction. Influenced by Journey, the potential of games as a medium for artistic expression and human connection became evident.

Foundation

Systematic Learning

Studying Software Engineering at Guangdong University of Foreign Studies provided a structured understanding of software architecture, networking, databases, and system design. This phase transformed a self-taught coder into someone capable of seeing the bigger picture in game development.

Practice

Experiments and Achievements

Hands-on projects like SimpleAnimator, ThatSkyInteractions Mod (TeaCon award-winning), and Phos (4C competition prototype) allowed testing of interaction mechanics, animation systems, and atmosphere-driven design. These milestones proved that technology can effectively serve design and player experience.

Exploration

Conceptual and Future Projects

Current and upcoming projects, including Animata4J, Tidal Carol, and Orbit, reflect ongoing experimentation with animation pipelines, temporal aesthetics, and philosophical themes. This stage represents the pursuit of innovation, expressive depth, and long-term vision in game creation.
